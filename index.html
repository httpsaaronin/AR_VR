How it Works

Nuclear power plants generate electricity using the heat created by nuclear fission. Inside the reactor core, atoms of uranium are split, releasing vast amounts of energy.

This heat boils water to produce steam, which spins massive turbines connected to electric generators.

Explore Five Key Areas:

  • ☢️ Reactor Building: See fission in action.
  • ⚙️ Turbine Hall: Watch steam generate power.
  • 🌊 Cooling Towers: Understand thermal regulation.
  • 🎛️ Control Room: Monitor plant operations.
  • 🚰 Piping Systems: Follow the energy flow.
